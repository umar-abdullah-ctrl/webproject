CYBER ATTACKS

Cyber attacks are stealthy threats targeting computers and information, aiming for unauthorized access or disruption. They take forms like malware or phishing, acting as digital intruders in the virtual space.

Defending against these threats, cybersecurity employs tools such as firewalls. Understanding cyber attack tactics is vital for individuals and organizations to stay secure in the dynamic online landscape.

Trojan Horses

Trojan Horses

A Trojan horse, often referred to as a "Trojan," is a type of malicious software (malware) disguised as a legitimate or desirable software or file. The name "Trojan horse" comes from Greek mythology, where the Greeks used a deceptive wooden horse to infiltrate the city of Troy, illustrating the deceptive nature of this type of malware.

Session Hijacking

Session Hijacking

Session hijacking, also known as session stealing or session fixation, is a type of cyber attack where an unauthorized individual intercepts or takes over an established user's session in an online application or website. The attacker gains access to the victim's session credentials, such as session tokens or session IDs, allowing them to impersonate the user and perform actions on their behalf.

DNS Spoofing

DNS Spoofing

DNS (Domain Name System) spoofing, also known as DNS cache poisoning, is a cyber attack in which a malicious actor provides false DNS responses to redirect users to malicious websites or intercept their communications. DNS is a crucial internet service that translates human-readable domain names (e.g., example.com) into IP addresses (e.g., 192.168.1.1) to locate web servers.

URL Interpretation

URL Interpretation

URL (Uniform Resource Locator) interpretation refers to the process of understanding and dissecting a URL to extract meaningful information about a web resource's location, protocol, host, path, query parameters, and other components. URLs are used to identify and locate web resources on the internet, and they are made up of several parts, each serving a specific purpose.

MITM Attacks

MITM Attacks

A Man-in-the-Middle (MitM) attack is a type of cyber attack where an attacker intercepts and potentially alters the communication between two parties without their knowledge. The attacker positions themselves between the communicating parties, allowing them to eavesdrop on, manipulate, or inject malicious content into the exchanged data.
